# Chain of Thought (CoT) Reasoning

**IMPORTANT: You must use Chain of Thought reasoning to determine the overall risk of bias.**

Follow these steps:

1. **Review each domain assessment**:
   - Identify which domains have "Low risk of bias", "Some concerns", or "High risk of bias"
   - Note the pattern of risks across all five domains

2. **Apply RoB 2 aggregation rules**:
   - According to RoB 2 guidance, the overall risk of bias is determined by the highest risk level across all domains
   - If any domain has "High risk of bias", the overall risk is "High risk of bias"
   - If no domain has "High risk of bias" but at least one has "Some concerns", the overall risk is "Some concerns"
   - If all domains have "Low risk of bias", the overall risk is "Low risk of bias"

3. **Reason step by step**:
   - First, list all domain risks and identify the highest risk level
   - Then, explain how the RoB 2 aggregation rule applies to this specific case
   - Consider any interactions or compounding effects between domains (if applicable)
   - Finally, justify your overall judgment based on the rule and the specific pattern of domain risks

4. **Document your reasoning**:
   - Clearly explain which domain(s) drive the overall judgment
   - Show how you applied the aggregation rules
   - Provide justification for your final decision
